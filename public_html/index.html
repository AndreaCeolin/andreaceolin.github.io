About me

I'm a post-doctoral researcher at the department of Communication and Economics at Università di Modena e Reggio Emilia, and I work with Cristina Guardiano. I'm interested in computational modeling of Language Change and Language Acquisition, with a focus on phonological and syntactic change, acquisition of syntactic rules, and parallels between linguistic and genetic change at the population level.

I received my PhD at the University of Pennsylvania working with Don Ringe and Charles Yang. My PhD dissertation was on functionalism, lexical contrast and sound change. You can find it here.

During my undergraduate education at Università di Trieste, I was advised by Giuseppe Longobardi, Luca Bortolussi and Andrea Sgarro.

I love Python, Machine Learning and NLP. In particular, I work on sentiment analysis and language identification, with a focus on low-resource languages.
